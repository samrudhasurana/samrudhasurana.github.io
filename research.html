Policy Paper

Powering Progress: Resolving Electricity Challenges Faced by India's Manufacturers

with Ankita Dinkar

Mercatus Research | Mercatus Center at George Mason University

December 2024

PDF

India's manufacturing sector faces persistent electricity challenges that impede growth and competitiveness. This paper examines the structural issues in India's electricity supply and proposes market-based solutions to resolve supply constraints, improve reliability, and reduce costs for manufacturers. We analyze policy reforms that could enhance the business environment and support India's manufacturing ambitions.

Working Paper

A Dissenting Voice: B.R. Shenoy's Austrian Economic Critique of India's Central Planning

with Vinayakan Sajeev-Beena

Also, a profile on B.R. Shenoy for Azim Premji University's Profiles on Indian Economists project.

B.R. Shenoy was one of India's most prominent economists and a lone dissenting voice against Nehruvian central planning in the 1950s and 1960s. This paper examines Shenoy's Austrian economic critique of India's planned economy, analyzing his theoretical contributions and policy recommendations. We explore how his insights align with modern Austrian economics and assess the relevance of his warnings for contemporary development policy.
